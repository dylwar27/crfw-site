# JANFEB11 PIRATE LIFE

**Project:** [[projects/alphabets]]  
**Series:** [[series/album-a-month]] — January–February 2011  
**File dates:** 2011-03-02 (slight overrun from Jan/Feb)  
**Archive location:** `_Documentation/Music/alphabets/JANFEB11 PIRATE LIFE`  
**Tracks:** 14  
**Format:** MP3  

A two-month album (January–February, echoing JANFEB09 from two years prior) with a pirate theme announced in the title. Pirate Life — the most directly themed of the 2011 albums. 14 tracks, filed March 2, indicating a late-month/early-next-month completion.

"aye pirate life" (track 07) is the album's explicit thematic declaration. But most tracks don't carry pirate imagery directly — the "pirate life" of the title may refer less to nautical piracy and more to the DIY/anti-establishment ethos: living outside the system, making art on your own terms. By January 2011, Colin had been releasing music independently for two years.

**"diamond body"** — a Buddhist/Tibetan concept (the vajra body, the body achieved through enlightened practice). The juxtaposition with "pirate life" — one celestial/spiritual, one rebellious/worldly — is characteristic.

**"ten pairs of tortoises"** — the most specific animal count in the catalog. Not "tortoises" but ten pairs, twenty tortoises.

"slowbook snowsnail" — the portmanteau returns: a snail made of snow, moving slowly through a book? Or a slow book, a snow snail?

"as of their wills" (track 14) closes the album: an unusual phrase — agency, volition, plurality.
