# MARCH09hopdanceshakebump

**Project:** [[projects/alphabets]]  
**Series:** [[series/album-a-month]] — Month 3  
**Release date:** March 2009  
**Archive location:** `_Documentation/Music/alphabets/MARCH09hopdanceshakebump`  
**Tracks:** 22  
**Format:** M4A  

The full album title embedded in the folder name — *hop dance shake bump* — is a four-verb instruction, a movement sequence, the album as choreography. 22 tracks in March 2009, still in M4A format.

The standout: **BOB ROSS RAVEGROOVE LOVE TRIBUTE** (track 15, the longest file at ~7MB/7+ minutes). A tribute to the painter Bob Ross rendered as a rave groove and a love letter simultaneously. This is the kind of title that could only come from somewhere completely genuine.

Other titles in this album establish patterns that persist through years of output: *kitchen gibbons shout at toy bulldozers* (animals + household objects + verbs), *laundry monster stomp contest* (the monster prefix that will define JUNE_MONSTERS_09), *eveninginginginginging* (phonetic repetition as title — the word "evening" extended until it becomes sound).

*R's synthgift to use* likely refers to a specific person ("R") who gave Colin a synthesizer or synth preset. *suave and debonair* is the most traditionally musical title here — two adjectives, no noun, more like a feeling.
