# SFTWRSYNTHZdec09

**Project:** [[projects/alphabets]]  
**Series:** [[series/album-a-month]] — Month 12 (second of two December 2009 releases)  
**Release date:** December 2009  
**Archive location:** `_Documentation/Music/alphabets/SFTWRSYNTHZdec09`  
**Tracks:** 8  
**Format:** MP3  

The title is an abbreviation: *Software Synths December 2009*. An album explicitly named for its tools — the software synthesizers Colin was using. This is the most self-referential release in the album-a-month catalog: the name is not a concept or a feeling but a production method.

8 tracks with a more abstract naming style than DEC09SCIFI: *open*, *cute helmet*, *flying*, *feet spots*, *wink practice*, *tuff helmet*, *fiction science*, *close*. The album structure is visible in the titles — *open* begins it and *close* ends it, a formal gesture toward the album as a unified arc.

"cute helmet" and "tuff helmet" are a pair — two helmets at different registers (tender vs. hard), like a call and response. "wink practice" implies repetition, rehearsal, a gesture being learned. "fiction science" inverts "science fiction" — putting the fiction first, the science secondary.

Two December 2009 albums in the same month — DEC09SCIFI and SFTWRSYNTHZdec09 — may represent two different modes Colin was working in simultaneously: the themed concept album (sci-fi) and the tool-specific abstract album (software synths).
