The track titles are almost entirely non-descriptive: *sounds 1* through *sounds 9* (with *sounds 2* absent, and the sequence non-sequential: sounds 7, 5, 4, 2, 1, 6, 3, 8, 9 in order). The opening track — *silence then outtro from meditation* — is the only evocative title and is substantially longer than the others (28MB vs. ~7MB each for the numbered tracks), suggesting it functions as a prelude/outro piece.

The numbering of the tracks doesn't match the order (sounds 7 comes before sounds 5 before sounds 4, etc.) — this may reflect the order they were composed rather than the intended playback order, or it may indicate the numbered tracks were created as a library and the soundtrack sequenced the *silence then outtro* piece as a primary element.

**"Life Before Time"**: What was this project? Possibilities include a short film, a live performance, a theatrical piece, or a personal artistic statement. The title has resonance — "life before time" suggests prehistory, eternity, or the imagination of a state before linear experience. Given Colin's 2009-2010 output, this could be connected to an early performance at Rhinoceropolis or another Denver DIY space.
