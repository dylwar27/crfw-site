# NOV09

**Project:** [[projects/alphabets]]  
**Series:** [[series/album-a-month]] — Month 11  
**Release date:** 2009-11-29  
**Archive location:** `_Documentation/Music/alphabets/NOV09`  
**Tracks:** 15 (16 files including one .asd session reference)  
**Format:** MP3  

The most historically significant of the monthly albums. NOV09 contains two track titles that would become defining names in Colin's legacy:

**"bliss body"** — A track here in November 2009 became the name of a memorial concert held in 2018 after Colin's death: *Bliss Body: A Tribute to Colin Ward*. The phrase originates here, nine years earlier.

**"run it run it fantasia mixx"** — The earliest known reference to *Fantasia* in the archive. Fantasia as a named event/concept dates to at least November 2009, predating the first vaulted Fantasia (2012) by over two years. The track name suggests Fantasia already existed as a running concept by this point — the "mixx" suffix indicating an ongoing series or recurring event rather than a one-time thing.

"run it run it hrt throb mixx" (track 15) is a parallel mix, suggesting NOV09 was being used as a space to develop the Fantasia sound: mix tracks named alongside the regular tracks.

15 tracks in total, the album's range moving from *nova brrrrr open* (the cold December ahead) through *shiva body* (Hindu deity of destruction; a body made sacred) to *puke up hugs* (physical intimacy and nausea held together), closing with the bliss body slow-down and the hrt throb mix.

## Archive Notes

- File dates: 2009-11-29
- **"bliss body"**: Track 8; nine years later became the name of *Bliss Body: A Tribute to Colin Ward* (2018 memorial event). The phrase almost certainly took its name from this track.
- **"run it run it fantasia mixx"**: Earliest archive evidence of Fantasia as a concept/event; predates the vaulted Fantasia 2012 by 3+ years
- *blssbdyslowdscrwd*: An abbreviated title — "bliss body slowed (crowd)" — suggesting a performance version or edit
- Duplicate track 04: both "bouncipears" and "moves bursts insides guts" carry the same track number; one may have replaced the other
- .asd file present for "shiva body" — session reference from a later (2016) Ableton session, suggesting Colin revisited this track years later
